From my perspective, culture is what brings a sense of community to a group of people by providing common values, beliefs and interests within this group. Within my family, we have a unique culture, one in which two cultures have merged to form the norms and values I learned growing up. My mother was born and raised in the US and my father was born in Mexico, bouncing back and forth from MX to the US throughout his childhood. Because of this I feel that I identify strongly with the Chicano culture/community. Culture in my family means honoring your familial history and background while being family oriented and treating each other with respect. When explaining who I am, where I come from and what I believe in I have always felt a sense of empowerment as I am proud of all of these things. Without my culture, I would not be the same woman I have grown to be; my entire identity revolves around it. I live my culture in various ways. Aside from the aforementioned aspects of my life, I also share my culture with those around me. In doing so I am helping keep my culture alive and teaching others the way I live my life.
